The top 5 most beautiful places

  1. Victoria Falls on the Zambezi River, the border of Zambia and Zimbabwe

  2. Yosemite Valley, USA

  3. Sagano Bamboo Forest in Kyoto, Japan

  4. Li River, China

  5. Salar de Uyuni – the world's largest salt marsh, Bolivia

Salar de Uyuni or Uyuni salt Desert is a dried-up reservoir and the largest salt marsh in the world.Once upon a time, Salar de Uyuni was an integral part of the prehistoric salt lake that occupied most of southwestern Bolivia. After the reservoir dried up, it turned into several small seasonal lakes and salt marshes. The unsurpassed beauty and atmosphere of this salt desert makes it one of the most impressive natural attractions in all of South America.

5. Salar de Uyuni – the world's largest salt marsh, Bolivia
The Li River is one of the most picturesque places in China. National Geographic magazine included the Lee River in the list of ten water wonders of the world. The Li River is often called the "pearl of China", and the residents of the city of Guilin, which is located on the banks of the river, consider it the soul and heart of their city. The river passes through the largest and most beautiful karst landscape in the world.

4. Li River, China
Sagano Bamboo Forest is an amazing bamboo grove in which thousands of thin and tall bamboo trees grow. The forest is one of the most picturesque natural attractions in Japan, and it is located in the western part of the city of Kyoto in a park called Arashiyama.

3.Sagano Bamboo Forest in Kyoto, Japan
Yosemite National Park is located on the western slopes of the Sierra Nevada Range in California. This ridge stretches across the whole of California, its height increases from west to east. One of the most popular objects in the world among rock climbers with routes of different difficulty levels is the granite rock El Capitan with a height of 2307 m above sea level, clearly visible from any part of the valley.

2. Yosemite Valley, USA
Majestic beauty and power, incessant hum for a second, a million splashes forming a cloud of steam and fog, a sense of danger when standing on the edge, and a dazzling rainbow above all this grandeur — this is exactly the picture you can observe at Victoria Falls, one of the largest waterfalls in the world.
